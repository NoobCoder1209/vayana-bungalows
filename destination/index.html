Tsarevo, Bulgaria

On the Black Sea coast

A small port town on Bulgaria's southern Black Sea coast, framed by the Strandzha pine forest and a long ribbon of quiet beaches.

About Tsarevo

Tsarevo sits at the southern end of the Bulgarian Black Sea coast, where the Strandzha National Park's pine ridges run all the way down to the water.

It's an old fishing port that grew slowly — small harbour, a single ridge, a tight grid of low-rise streets — and the seasonality is gentle. The beaches stretch in both directions: Veleka and Sinemorets to the south, the sweep of Tsarevo bay to the north, and a string of unnamed coves in between.

It's reachable by car in a little over an hour from Burgas Airport (BOJ, ~85 km) and by a long drive from Sofia (SOF, ~410 km). Most guests fly into BOJ; the route hugs the coast from Burgas south, with the sea on the left almost all the way down.

The shoreline

Beaches and coves

Long open beaches give way to small, half-hidden coves the further south you walk. Veleka, just down the coast, is one of the cleanest river-mouth beaches in the country; the smaller bays north of town are reachable only on foot or by water and rarely crowded, even in August.

On the table

Restaurants and local food

Most of what's good is within walking distance of the harbour: fresh Black Sea catch, mussels and rapana from the bay, and a handful of small family kitchens that serve mezze under grapevines. A few inland wineries in the Strandzha foothills run tasting days through the warm months.

Inland

Hiking and nature

Strandzha National Park — the largest protected area in Bulgaria — begins at the edge of town and runs inland for tens of kilometres. Marked trails follow the river valleys; the migratory bird routes overhead in spring and autumn are reason enough for a long walk, even if you don't normally count yourself a birder.

Around the coast

Nearby villages and culture

Sinemorets and Ahtopol are short drives down the coast, each with its own character — Sinemorets quieter and built around its river-mouth beach, Ahtopol with a working harbour and a small old town on a headland. Inland, Strandzha villages keep older traditions alive, including the fire-dancing rituals that the region is known for.
