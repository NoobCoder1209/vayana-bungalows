Privacy Policy

Last updated: 22 June 2026

This page explains what personal data Vayana Bungalows collects, why we collect it, how long we keep it, and what rights you have over it under the EU General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and Bulgarian data-protection law.

1. Last updated

This Privacy Policy was last updated on 22 June 2026. See §12 Changes to this policy for how we handle future updates.

2. Who we are

We are Vayana Bungalows, operated by Vayana di Mare, a Bulgarian hospitality business operating under licence number 1327673.

Our registered location is ul. Kraybrezhna 1, Tsarevo, Bulgaria. For any privacy-related question you can reach us by email at contact@vayanabungalows.com.

For the purposes of GDPR, we are the data controller for any personal data submitted through this website.

3. What data we collect

We only collect personal data that you actively provide to us, plus a small amount of technical data that our anti-spam tools need to operate. We do not run any analytics, advertising, or behavioural-tracking tools on this site.

3.1 Data you give us through the enquiry form

The enquiry form on /enquiries/ collects the following fields. All are submitted only when you choose to submit the form:

  • Full name
  • Email address
  • Phone number
  • Preferred check-in and check-out dates
  • Number of guests (adults, children, infants)
  • A free-text message describing your enquiry
  • A consent checkbox confirming you agree to be contacted (this is logged together with the submission, so we have a record of your consent — see §4)

The form currently does not submit data anywhere — version 1 of the page opens a "thank you" modal and discards what you typed. When the submission endpoint ships, this section will be updated in the same release — see §12 Changes to this policy.

3.2 Technical data via anti-spam (reCAPTCHA / Turnstile)

When the enquiry submission endpoint ships, we plan to add a captcha challenge (Google reCAPTCHA v3 or Cloudflare Turnstile — decision pending). The captcha provider will collect, on Google's or Cloudflare's behalf:

  • Your IP address (truncated to the first three octets, i.e. /24, before storage on our side)
  • Browser characteristics (user agent, screen size, plug-in list)
  • Interaction signals on the form page (mouse / keyboard movement timing)

This data does not pass through our servers in clear form — it goes directly from your browser to the captcha provider, which returns only a risk score to us.

3.3 Cookies and tracking from embedded Google Maps

The map on the homepage and on Contacts / Destination embeds a Google Maps iframe. When the page loads the iframe, Google may set cookies in your browser and receive your IP address — this happens automatically as soon as the page is rendered, whether or not you interact with the map. We have no control over what Google collects in that context — please see Google's own privacy policy.

3.4 Newsletter signup

The newsletter signup form on the homepage accepts an email address into an input field but, in version 1, that email is not stored anywhere and is not transmitted off your device — submitting the form opens a "thank you" modal and discards what you typed. When a real email-service-provider integration ships, this section will be updated in the same release to list the provider and what they receive.

4. Why we collect it (legal basis)

  • Your consent (GDPR Art. 6(1)(a)). The enquiry form processes your data on the basis of the consent you give by ticking the checkbox above the submit button. You can withdraw that consent at any time — see §9.
  • Our legitimate interest (GDPR Art. 6(1)(f)). The captcha service that runs alongside the form processes the technical data listed in §3.2 to protect our site from automated spam submissions. We have weighed this interest against your rights and consider it proportionate, since the data captured is the minimum required to score a request as bot-or-human.

5. How we use it

We use the data you give us solely to:

  • Reply to your enquiry with a tailored proposal
  • Hold a record of your stated preferences in case you reply later in the same thread
  • Detect and block spam submissions (captcha)

We do not use enquiry data for marketing. We do not share it with advertising networks. We do not run any automated decision-making or profiling on it.

6. Third parties

When you use this site, the following third parties may process some of your personal data on our behalf:

  • Google LLC — for the embedded Google Maps iframe (§3.3) and, if we choose it as the captcha, Google reCAPTCHA v3 (§3.2). Google's processing is governed by its own privacy policy.
  • Cloudflare, Inc. — for hosting the form submission endpoint when it ships (#15), and, if we choose it as the captcha, Cloudflare Turnstile (§3.2). Cloudflare's processing is governed by its own privacy policy.
  • GitHub, Inc. — this website is served as a static site through GitHub Pages. GitHub receives your IP address and basic request metadata in the course of serving the page. GitHub's processing is governed by its own privacy statement.

7. Where data is stored

The static website itself contains no stored personal data. Once the enquiry submission endpoint ships:

  • Enquiry submissions will pass through Cloudflare's globally distributed edge network
  • Persisted submissions will live in storage configured to keep data within the European Union where possible

The exact storage architecture is still being designed; this section will be made more specific in the same release that ships submission.

8. How long we keep it

We keep enquiry data for the minimum time necessary to respond to you and follow up. Our current target is to delete enquiry records 12 months after the last interaction in that thread.

If your enquiry becomes a booking, the booking-related data is retained under our booking terms (not this policy). If your enquiry does not become a booking, the 12-month window applies.

The "12 months" figure is provisional and will be confirmed before launch.

9. Your rights

Under GDPR, you have the following rights over the data we hold about you. To exercise any of them, email us at contact@vayanabungalows.com and we will respond within one calendar month.

  • Access (Art. 15) — ask us for a copy of what we hold about you
  • Rectification (Art. 16) — ask us to correct inaccurate or incomplete data
  • Erasure (Art. 17) — ask us to delete your data, where one of the grounds in Art. 17(1) applies
  • Restriction (Art. 18) — ask us to pause processing while we investigate a dispute
  • Portability (Art. 20) — ask us to send you (or another controller) a machine-readable copy
  • Object (Art. 21) — object to processing carried out on the basis of legitimate interest (§4)
  • Withdraw consent (Art. 7(3)) — withdraw the consent you gave on the enquiry form at any time, by emailing us. Withdrawing consent does not affect the lawfulness of any processing carried out before the withdrawal.

You also have the right to lodge a complaint with the Bulgarian Commission for Personal Data Protection (CPDP) if you believe we have mishandled your data.

10. Cookies

We do not set any first-party cookies on this site. Cookies are only set by embedded third-party content:

  • Google Maps — sets cookies and receives your IP address when the embedded map iframe loads on the homepage, the Contacts page, and the Destination page; see Google's own cookie disclosures
  • Captcha provider — once the captcha ships (#20), it will set strictly necessary cookies to prevent spam; the specific cookies depend on whether we choose Google reCAPTCHA or Cloudflare Turnstile

We do not run any analytics, advertising, or behavioural-tracking cookies. If that changes — for example if we add a newsletter analytics tool — we will add a cookie banner before doing so and update this section.

11. How to contact us about data

For anything related to your data — access requests, corrections, deletions, complaints, or just questions about this policy — please email us:

  • Email: contact@vayanabungalows.com
  • Postal address: ul. Kraybrezhna 1, Tsarevo, Bulgaria

We aim to acknowledge data-related emails within five working days, and to substantively reply within one calendar month, as GDPR requires.

12. Changes to this policy

We may update this policy from time to time — for example when we add a new third-party service, change our retention schedule, or in response to a regulatory update. Whenever we update it, we change the "last updated" date at the top.

For material changes (anything that affects what we collect, why we collect it, or how long we keep it), we will additionally announce the change on the homepage for a reasonable period of time so that returning visitors notice.
